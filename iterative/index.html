Iterative Design

Overview

In this project, I worked with a group to design an application for a new startup. We then improved upon our original design in response to feedback from peers and user testing.

The startup we selected was Wolfia, a company that allows developers of mobile apps to easily get feedback on their work by sharing their project with a simple link.

Process

First, we individually created quick sketches for several of the application's interfaces to explore a variety of layouts and potential design decisions.

These sketches transformed into a set of wireframes for the most important interfaces of the application.

Finally, we produced a high fidelity, interactive prototype featuring most of the application's screens. This prototype was reviewed by other students in the course to help us improve upon the design.

Additionally, we sent the prototype to user testers and instructed them to imagine they were an app developer attempting to find feedback left on their most recent version of the app. We observed where they struggled to interpret an interface or got lost navigating through the application, which helped us make further improvements to the next iteration of our prototype.

Result

Several rounds of feedback and user testing helped polish the final version of our prototype, which you can view here.

Skills Developed

  • Identifying the best features of several initial design ideas and blending them into a single prototype.
  • Refining a design in response to valuable criticism.
  • Crafting user testing instructions that will generate relevant and informative results.
